Fathima Beevi Obituary: How Did She Die? India First Judge Cause Of Death

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, September 11, 2024

Fathima Beevi Death News: Everyone was taken aback by the death announcement. M. Fathima Beevi was a Supreme Court of India judge. When she was appointed to the Supreme Court of India in 1989, she was the first Muslim woman appointed to any of the country’s higher courts and the first female judge. Following her resignation from the court, she served as a commissioner on the National Human Rights Commission and as governor of Tamil Nadu, an Indian state, from 1997 to 2001.

The Kerala Prabha Award, the second-highest accolade conferred by the Kerala government, was bestowed to her in 2023. Beevi was admitted as an Advocate on November 14, 1950. She passed the Bar Council test in 1950. Her career began in the lower courts of Kerala. She was appointed Munsiff of the Kerala Subordinate Judicial Services in May 1958. She was promoted to the rank of Subordinate Judge in 1968, Chief Judicial Magistrate in 1972, and District & Sessions Judge in 1974. Let us continue reading to understand more about Fathima Beevi’s death and other information.

Fathima Beevi Obituary

Justice Fathima Beevi, the first female judge of the Indian Supreme Court, died on Thursday at the age of 96. After leaving the Supreme Court, the Justice was named Governor of Tamil Nadu. Kerala’s Health Minister Veena George expressed her sympathies on Beevi’s death and stated it was a tragic death.

According to the minister, Justice Beevi created history as the first female governor of Tamil Nadu and a Supreme Court judge. She was a brave lady from a prestigious family. “She was a personality who, through her own life showed that willpower and a sense of purpose can overcome any adversity,” she said in a statement. Fathima Beevi’s death is a significant loss for the Indian legal community and those who admire her. Her family has yet to provide information on her obituary.

How Did India’s First Judge Pass Away?

According to official reports, Justice Fathima Beevi, a former governor of Tamil Nadu and the Supreme Court’s first female judge died on Thursday in a private hospital in this city. At the time of her death, she was 96 years old. State health minister Veena George offered her sympathies on Justice Beevi’s death, saying it was a horrible tragedy but without specifying the reason.

M. Fathima Beevi, the daughter of Khadeeja Beevi and Annaveettil Meer Sahib, was born on April 30, 1927, into a rowdy family in Pathanamthitta, Kingdom of Travancore, now part of Kerala, India. She earned a Bachelor of Science in Chemistry from University College in Thiruvananthapuram after attending Pathanamthitta’s Town School and Catholicate High School. Fathima Beevi earned a B.L. from the Government Law College in Thiruvananthapuram. She was appointed Governor of Tamil Nadu on January 25, 1997. Shankar Dayal Sharma, then-President of India, appointed her as Governor of Tamil Nadu and Justice Sukhdev Singh Kang, former Chief Justice of the Jammu and Kashmir High Court, as Governor of Kerala, stating that “their experience of and insights into the workings of the Constitution and the laws comprise valuable assets.”

She dismissed the four condemned convicts’ appeals for compassion in the Rajiv Gandhi murder case in her role as state governor. The prisoners’ compassion petitions to the Governor requested her to use her powers under Article 161 of the Constitution.
