Francis J. Lorango Obituary, North Tonawanda, Former President Of The Exchange Club Of The Tonawanda

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, September 17, 2024

Remembering the Life of Francis J. Lorango: A Community Leader and Music Enthusiast. On Monday, November 13, 2023, North Tonawanda bid farewell to Francis J. Lorango, affectionately known as “Frankie.” At the age of 97, Frankie’s passing marked the end of an era for the community. As a former US Navy serviceman, Frankie’s life was filled with remarkable achievements and contributions that left an indelible mark on those around him. From his role as a beloved father and grandfather to his dedication as a community leader and music director, Frankie’s legacy will be remembered for generations to come. Francis J. Lorango Obituary

It is with deep sadness that we announce the passing of Francis J. Lorango, affectionately known as “Frankie,” on Monday, November 13, 2023. At the age of 97, Frankie peacefully left this world in North Tonawanda. He was the former husband of Martha Keller Lorango and the loving fiancé of Anne Trane. Frankie was a cherished father to Nancy Hook and Gene F. Lorango, and a proud grandfather to Jennifer, Megan, Blake, and Angeline. He also had the joy of being a great-grandfather to Lianna, Ryland, Arlo, Lukas, and Oscar. Frankie will be deeply missed by his family and all who knew him.

Death and Family

Frankie’s passing has left a void in the hearts of his loved ones. He was a beloved brother to Mary Vincent Lorango and is survived by several cousins and a nephew. His family and friends gathered to pay their respects at the Brunner Funeral Home in North Tonawanda. A Memorial Mass with Military Honors was held at Our Lady of Czestochowa Parish, where Frankie was remembered and honored for his remarkable life. He was laid to rest in a private burial at Elmlawn Cemetery, surrounded by the love and memories of those who cherished him.

Professional Life and Accomplishments

Frankie’s professional journey was marked by his passion for bicycles. After serving as a Seaman First Class in the US Navy during World War II, he became a Huffy and Schwinn bike dealer. For nearly four decades, Frankie’s Twin City Cyclery served the community of Tonawanda. In addition to his bike shop, Frankie also owned a slot car shop, ceramic studio, Frankie’s Fast Speedway, and a hobby shop. His dedication to his craft and his commitment to providing quality service made him a respected figure in the industry.

Community Involvement and Hobbies

Frankie’s impact extended beyond his professional life. He was the director of the SUGAR & JAZZ Orchestra, where he played a vital role in helping local students appreciate and embrace the “Big Band Sound.” Frankie’s passion for music and his dedication to teaching inspired countless middle and high school students. Alongside his former wife, Martie, he spent countless hours on band practice, performances, and educational excursions. Their compassionate and firm guidance created a joyful and community-focused environment for young musicians to develop their skills.

Outside of his musical endeavors, Frankie was actively involved in his community. He served as the president of the Tonawanda Exchange Club on two occasions and held the position of president in the WNY National Bicycle Dealers Association. In recognition of his outstanding leadership and dedication, the Tonawanda Chamber of Commerce named him Citizen of the Year in 2000. Frankie’s commitment to his community and his passion for making a difference were truly commendable.

Memorial and Donations

In honor of Frankie’s memory, the family kindly requests donations to the Community Music School of Buffalo. Contributions can be made to the school at 217 East Delavan Avenue, Buffalo, NY 14208. By supporting this organization, we can continue to foster the love and appreciation for music that Frankie held dear throughout his life. Your generosity will help ensure that future generations have the opportunity to experience the joy and transformative power of music, just as Frankie did.

We mourn the passing of Francis J. Lorango, affectionately known as “Frankie,” who peacefully left us on November 13, 2023, at the age of 97 in North Tonawanda. He was a beloved father, grandfather, and great-grandfather. Frank served in the US Navy during WWII and later became a prominent figure in his community as a bike dealer and orchestra director. His dedication to music education and community service will be remembered fondly. Our thoughts and condolences go out to his family and loved ones during this difficult time. In honor of Frank, the family kindly requests donations to the Community Music School of Buffalo.
