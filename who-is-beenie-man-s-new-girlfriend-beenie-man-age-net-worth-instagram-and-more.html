Who is  Beenie Man?

Born Moses Anthony Davis, Beenie Man stands as a pivotal name in reggae and dancehall music, garnering accomplishment and influence.

His journey commenced at the tender age of eight, clinching victory in a talent competition and producing his inaugural track, “Too Fancy.”

The debut album, “The Invincible Beenie Man: The Ten Year Old DJ Wonder,” surfaced in 1983.

His zenith materialized during the 1990s with chart-toppers such as:

  • “Who Am I (Sim Simma),”
  • “Girls Dem Sugar,”
  • “Feel It Boy,”
  • “Dude.”

    • Collaborations with luminaries like Wyclef Jean, Mýa, Kelis, Lil Jon, and Janet Jackson embellish his repertoire.

    The crowning jewel of his accolades arrived in the form of a Grammy for Best Reggae Album in 2000, attributed to “Art and Life.”

    His prolific productivity and widespread appeal have rightfully cemented his status as the King of Dancehall.
