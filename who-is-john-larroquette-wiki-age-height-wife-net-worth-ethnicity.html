John Larroquette Biography

John Edgar Larroquette Jr., worked for the United States Navy and was born on November 25, 1947, in New Orleans, Louisiana. Berthalla Oramous Helmstetter, his mother, worked as a clerk in a department store. His paternal grandfather was a French immigrant who arrived in the United States in 1895.

Throughout his childhood, John played the saxophone and clarinet, and in high school, he and his friends formed a band called NUDLES. He attended Holy Cross School, Francis T. Nicholls High School, and finally Fredrick Douglass Senior High School, where he discovered his acting talent during his senior year. After finishing his studies, he moved to Hollywood in 1973 to pursue a career as an actor.

Career

John’s acting career began in 1973 when he relocated to Hollywood. His first role was as the opening narrator in the play The Texas Chain Saw Massacre. On NBC, he had his first series role in the show Baa Baa Black Sheep. He made an appearance in the 1975 film Sanford and Son.

From 1984 to 1992, he played Dan Fielding in the television series Night Court. With his appearances on the show, he broke the record of winning the Emmys four times in a row. John appeared in a few episodes of The Practice in 1998.

From 2004 to 2006, John narrated the remake of The Texas Chainsaw Massacre. The following year, he joined the cast of Boston Legal and appeared as a guest star in the drama House. In Phineas and Ferb, John provided his voice.

He became a regular on the television show The Librarians, which helped him gain popularity among the audience. In 2019, he had a recurring role in the television series Blood & Treasure, where he played the main character’s father.

He has appeared in films such as Second Sight, Blind Date, Summer Rental, Star Trek 111, Richie Rich, Demon Knight, and Meatballs Part 11. He has also appeared in plays such as How the Grinch Stole Christmas, The Best Man, and How to Succeed in Business Without Really Trying. In 2019, he appeared in the play Nantucket Sleigh Ride.
